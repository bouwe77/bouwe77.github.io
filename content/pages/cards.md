# My approach to collaboration

I value the perspective I don’t have yet.

Expertise is only useful if it remains flexible. I have been in my profession long enough to build a deep foundation, but I make a conscious effort to ensure my experience never becomes a barrier to new ideas. I believe that every person I encounter—regardless of their role or history—offers a vantage point that I simply cannot see from where I am standing. I thrive on the reciprocal nature of work. I am always ready to share what I know, but I am just as eager to let a fresh insight change my mind.

# My philosophy on testing

I test requirements, not implementation.

A test suite should be a safety net for refactoring, not an anchor that holds code in place. When we test implementation details, we lock the code into its current form, making evolution unnecessarily painful and brittle. I focus my energy on the "what"—the actual requirements and user outcomes—rather than the "how." By prioritizing integration and E2E tests over granular unit tests, I ensure the software actually does what it’s supposed to do for the user. This approach allows me to refactor and improve the internal structure with confidence, knowing that if the specs are met, the tests will stay green.
